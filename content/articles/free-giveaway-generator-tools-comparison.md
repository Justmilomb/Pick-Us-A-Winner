## The "Free" Hidden Costs to Watch For

### 1. Your Data Being Sold
Some free tools make money by selling:
- Your entrants' email addresses to advertisers
- Engagement data to analytics companies
- Participant lists to marketing platforms

**How to check:** Read the privacy policy. Look for phrases like "third-party advertising partners" or "data monetisation."

---

### 2. Your Audience's Data Being Harvested
If the tool requires your followers to sign up or enter emails, check if those emails are being captured by the tool (not just you).

---

### 3. Fake Randomness
Some tools use JavaScript's `Math.random()`, which is a pseudo-random number generator. It's predictable if you know the algorithm.

**The risk:** A technically-savvy person could theoretically predict results. For fair, verifiable giveaways, use cryptographic randomness.

---

### 4. Comment Limits
Many free tools cap at 100-500 comments. If your post has 1,000+ comments, you'll need to pay or the tool will only sample a subset — potentially biasing results toward early commenters.
